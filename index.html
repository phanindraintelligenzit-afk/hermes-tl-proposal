A proposal for PVR

Give every team lead an assistant that actually knows them.

Not a chat bot. Not a spreadsheet. An agent that listens, learns, and takes care of the things that shouldn't need a team lead's attention.

Every team lead we have is someone who earned their way up because they're good at their craft. They know the architecture. They know the client. They know their team.

But most of their day isn't spent on any of that. It's spent on chasing attendance, compiling reports, summarizing standups, answering the same status questions over and over.

Not because they want to. Because someone has to do it. And that someone is them.

What if the team lead could focus on the work that actually needs them — and everything else just happened?

That's what this is about. Not a tool they have to learn. An agent that learns them.

How it works

When a new team lead gets Hermes, it doesn't come with a manual. It comes with a conversation.

It asks: "What does your team do? Who's on it? What does a typical day look like? What tasks do you find yourself repeating every week?"

The team lead answers. That's it. The agent remembers everything and starts looking for ways to help.

A moment

"I can take care of attendance for you."

After the onboarding conversation, Hermes understands the team's schedule. It starts tracking logins, flagging latecomers, and delivering a daily summary to the team lead's Slack — without being asked.

A moment

"Here's your EOD report, ready to send."

At the end of the day, Hermes collects updates from the team, compiles them into a clean report, and drops it in the team lead's DMs. One tap to approve. Zero copy-paste.

A moment

"Your team is asking about the deployment."

Team members message Hermes directly: "What's the status on the deployment?" "Who's handling the client escalation?" "When's the next standup?" — answers are instant, even when the team lead is in a meeting.

None of these require the team lead to configure anything. The agent proposes. The team lead says yes or no. That's the whole interaction.

Why this matters

The team leads we have are the people we trust the most. They're also the ones with the least amount of uninterrupted time.

Every hour they spend on operational overhead is an hour they're not spending on the work that grows the team, serves the client, or moves the project forward.

And when a team lead leaves — which happens, because burnout is real — all that knowledge walks out the door. The next person starts from zero.

Hermes preserves what the team lead knows. The workflows. The SOPs. The team context. When someone new steps in, the agent is already there, ready to bring them up to speed.

The team doesn't lose its memory when someone leaves. It stays.
What we're asking

Just the time to try it with one person.

Pick one team lead. Set up a profile. Have them talk to Hermes for 30 minutes. See what happens.

If they find it useful, we do the next one. If not, we learn and adjust. There's nothing to buy, nothing to install, nothing to risk.

We're already running Hermes. Every team lead getting their own profile is just a matter of creating the space for them to have that first conversation.

This is a proposal to try something small and see if it works. Not a pitch for a big initiative. One team lead. One conversation. See what happens.

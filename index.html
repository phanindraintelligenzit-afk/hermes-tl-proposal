Give each team lead at IntelligenzIT an assistant that actually knows their team — and takes care of the things that shouldn't need their attention.

The Problem

Our team leads are the people we trust the most. They're also the ones with the least uninterrupted time.

📋

Attendance tracking

Every morning: who's in, who's late, who's on leave. Every evening: who logged what. Someone has to do it. That someone is the team lead.

📝

End-of-day reports

Each team member writes an update. The TL compiles, formats, and forwards it upward. Hours of copy-paste work every week.

🗣️

Standup aggregation

Reading updates from everyone, summarizing for management, chasing missing entries. Necessary. Repetitive. Time-consuming.

🔍

Ad-hoc queries

"What's the status on X?" "Who's working on Y?" "Is the blocker resolved?" — constant context-switching that breaks deep work.

None of these require a team lead's expertise. But they take up nearly half the week.

The Vision

Not a tool they have to learn. An agent that learns them.

💬

Onboarding is a conversation

Hermes asks: "What does your team do? Who are the members? What does a typical day look like?" The team lead answers in plain English. That's it. No training data, no config, no coding.

🧠

It remembers everything

Every name, role, workflow, SOP, and decision — saved permanently. It builds skills from experience and gets better over time. When a team lead leaves, their Hermes profile transfers to the next person. Nothing is lost.

⚡

Automations run silently

Attendance tracking, EOD reports, standup summaries — set up once during onboarding, then delivered to the team lead's Slack every day without them lifting a finger.

👥

Available to the whole team

Team members can message Hermes directly: "Who's on leave?" "What's the blocker?" "Help me draft this email." Answers are instant, even when the TL is offline.

What it looks like

Three moments from a team lead's day.

Morning

"I can take care of attendance for you."

After the onboarding conversation, Hermes understands the team's schedule. It starts tracking logins, flagging latecomers, and delivering a daily summary to the team lead's Slack — without being asked.

Evening

"Here's your EOD report, ready to send."

At the end of the day, Hermes collects updates from the team, compiles them into a clean report, and drops it in the team lead's DMs. One tap to approve. Zero copy-paste.

Any time

"Your team is asking about the deployment."

Team members message Hermes directly: "What's the status?" "Who's handling the escalation?" Answers are instant — even when the TL is in back-to-back meetings.

How it works

Simple enough that anyone can do it.

STEP 01

Create a profile

A new Hermes profile for the team lead. Isolated memory, skills, and config. Takes five minutes.

STEP 02

30-minute conversation

The team lead talks to their Hermes. Answers questions about their team and workflows. The agent learns and saves everything.

STEP 03

Set up automations

Hermes proposes: "I can track attendance and compile reports. Want me to?" The TL approves. It runs from day one.

STEP 04

Connect to the team

Hermes joins the team's Slack channel. Team members can message it. Reports arrive automatically. The TL reclaims their time.

The problem is time

Team leads are our most expensive resource — and they're spending nearly half their week on things that don't need their expertise.

~3 hrs/week

Attendance

Logging in, logging out, chasing latecomers, preparing reports. Every team lead does this. None of them should.

~4 hrs/week

End-of-Day Reports

Each team member writes one. The TL compiles, formats, and forwards. Copy-paste work that takes hours.

~3 hrs/week

Standups & Status

Reading updates, summarizing for management, chasing missing entries. It adds up.

~5 hrs/week

Ad-Hoc Queries

"What's the status on X?" "Who's working on Y?" "Is the blocker resolved?" — constant context-switching.

That's roughly 15 hours per week per team lead spent on tasks that don't require their judgment.
For 5 team leads: 75 hours a week — the equivalent of two full-time people.

What if each team lead had an assistant that never sleeps?

Not a tool they have to learn. An agent that learns them.

Through conversation

Onboarding takes 30 minutes

Hermes asks: "What does your team do? Who are the members? What does a typical day look like? What tasks do you repeat every week?" The team lead answers in plain English. That's it. No training data, no config, no coding.

Across every session

It remembers everything

Names, roles, workflows, SOPs, decisions — all saved permanently. It builds skills from experience and gets better over time. When a team lead leaves, their Hermes profile transfers to the next person. Nothing is lost.

On autopilot

Automations run forever

Attendance tracking, EOD reports, standup summaries, daily digests — set up once during onboarding, then delivered to the TL's Slack every day without them lifting a finger.

For the whole team

24/7 team assistant

Team members can ask Hermes anything: "Who's on leave?", "What's the blocker on Project X?", "Help me draft this email." Answers are instant, even when the TL is offline.

Here's exactly what happens

Four steps, no surprises.

01

Create a profile

We create a new Hermes profile for the team lead. A profile is a directory with isolated memory, skills, and config. Takes 5 minutes.

02

30-minute onboarding

The team lead chats with their Hermes. Answers questions about their team, workflows, and pain points. The agent learns and saves everything.

03

Set up automations

Hermes proposes: "I can track attendance and compile EOD reports. Want me to?" The TL approves. Cron jobs run from day one.

04

Connect to Slack

Hermes joins the team's Slack channel. Team members message it directly. Reports arrive without the TL lifting a finger.
